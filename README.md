Overall Insights

* Payment methods are roughly evenly distributed, with each method holding about 15-17% share and counts clustered around 612–677, indicating no dominant method and diverse payment preferences.

* Non-subscribers outnumber subscribers across all purchase frequency bands by about 2.5–3x, with the largest non-subscriber counts at less frequent intervals (Every 3 Months, Quarterly, Annually) suggesting sporadic shopping by non-subscribers.

* Subscribers show steadier purchase frequencies with modest peaks at Annual and Weekly intervals, indicating a mix of regular and long-term subscribers.

* Clothing is the largest contributor to seasonal purchase totals in every season, roughly $23K–$27K, driving overall volume.

* Fall has the highest total purchases overall, with notably high contributions from accessories and clothing, while Summer is the lowest, indicating a fall-focused seasonal demand peak.

* Footwear and Outerwear contribute smaller but steady shares each season, implying consistent but lower demand compared with clothing and accessories.

* Male customers spend substantially more across all categories, with the largest gap in Clothing (approximately $70.6K for males vs $33.6K for females) and Accessories.

* The gender gap is most pronounced in Clothing and Accessories, suggesting opportunities to grow female spending in these categories through targeted marketing or assortment adjustments.

* Footwear and Outerwear show smaller absolute totals but follow the same pattern of higher male spend, indicating a consistent gender-based spending difference across product types.
